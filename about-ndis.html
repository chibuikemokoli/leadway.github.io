About The NDIS

NDIS

What is the NDIS?

The NDIS, short for the National Disability Insurance Scheme, is an Australian government initiative designed to provide support and funding to people with disabilities

Its goal is to empower individuals to live more independently and enhance their quality of life.

The NDIS achieves this by offering financial assistance for necessary services and supports tailored to each participant's unique needs.

If you have a disability, the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) could help you get the support you need to improve your skills, quality of life, and independence.

Achieve your goals with help from the NDIS


The NDIS is all about choice and control. If you have a disability, or you care for a person with a disability, the NDIS is designed to give you:


  • More choice around the supports you get
  • More control and flexibility around how and when those supports are provided
  • A lifetime approach to the delivery of disability support services — which means your support plan will change over time as your needs and goals change
  • More opportunities for early intervention — which means you get support before the point of crisis, which can help reduce the impact of disability
